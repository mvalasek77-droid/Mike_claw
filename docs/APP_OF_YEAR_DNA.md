# App of the Year DNA

CodeGenie treats Apple's last decade of iPhone App of the Year winners as
a product-quality benchmark, not a visual mood board.

## Winners Studied

| Year | iPhone winner | CodeGenie lesson |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 2025 | Tiimo | Make a stressful routine visually calm and actionable. |
| 2024 | Kino | Turn pro capture into something that feels native to iPhone. |
| 2023 | AllTrails | Build real-world utility around community and exploration. |
| 2022 | BeReal | Create a simple, repeatable authenticity ritual. |
| 2021 | Toca Life World | Give users a flexible world for self-expression. |
| 2020 | Wakeout! | Fit a useful health action into ordinary life. |
| 2019 | Spectre Camera | Use intelligence to simplify an advanced creative technique. |
| 2018 | Procreate Pocket | Compress professional creative power into a phone-first surface. |
| 2017 | Calm | Own a focused wellness ritual with emotional clarity. |
| 2016 | Prisma | Transform ordinary user input into distinctive art. |

## Segments

1. Calm utility: reduce friction, anxiety, or cognitive load immediately.
2. Creator superpower: make an advanced skill feel effortless on iPhone.
3. Human connection: give users a reason to share, return, or build identity.
4. Native craft: use Apple-specific hardware, frameworks, haptics, accessibility, or ecosystem surfaces.
5. Store story: prove the payoff with icon, screenshots, metadata, privacy, and TestFlight readiness.

## Product Enforcement

- Home exposes an "Award DNA" surface so the launch benchmark is visible.
- Architect, Designer, and Reviewer prompts require award-caliber thinking.
- Perfection Mode includes an `award_caliber` axis that flags missing
  first-run payoff, human outcome, native leverage, store story, or
  screenshots before TestFlight.
